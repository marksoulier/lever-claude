# FOUNDER.md — Mark Soulier

For use in: marketing copy, press, About page, investor conversations, community posts. Captures who Mark is, why he built Lever, and how he sounds.

---

## Who He Is

Mark Soulier is the founder of Lever. He studied Electrical Engineering at Utah State University, where he worked as a robotics researcher — building ROS2 systems, writing Arduino controllers, simulating autonomous vehicles in Gazebo. He went on to write Python tools that automated the analysis of biometric data at the Analytic Solution Center and tested defense software at Space Dynamics Laboratory.

He's an engineer in the original sense: someone who sees a complex system, figures out how it works, and builds a tool to give people leverage over it.

Lever is his side project. He's building it because he needed it himself.

---

## The Origin

Mark started building Lever when he was trying to make real financial decisions for himself — the kind every person in their 20s and 30s eventually faces. Job offers. Renting vs. buying. Retirement accounts. Whether to pay down debt or invest.

He found the same thing everyone finds: either you pay a financial advisor (who may not have your interests at heart), or you're left alone with a spreadsheet that's out of date two weeks after you make it.

Neither felt right. So he built the thing he wanted.

---

## The Belief

Mark's thesis is simple and it runs deeper than the product:

**When people aren't burdened by financial survival, they build the best things.**

Financial anxiety is one of the most effective brakes on human potential. It keeps people in jobs they hate, cities they've outgrown, and lives that feel smaller than they should. It stops people from taking risks, starting companies, making art, raising families the way they want to.

A person who understands their financial future — who can see the numbers, run the scenarios, and know they're on track — makes different decisions. Bolder ones. More generous ones.

Lever exists to give that clarity to everyone, not just people with enough money to hire a wealth manager.

## Voice & Tone (for marketing copy)

Mark doesn't talk like a fintech company. He talks like someone who built a thing for himself and thinks you should have it too. Some reference points:

- **Direct.** No hedge words. "This builds your financial plan through conversation" not "our platform leverages AI to facilitate personalized financial modeling."
- **Builder-to-builder.** Respects the user's intelligence. Doesn't over-explain.
- **Quietly confident.** The product does something real. Doesn't need to oversell.
- **Mission-grounded without being preachy.** The financial freedom belief shows up in what he builds, not in speeches about it.

**Write like this:**
> "I built Lever because I kept trying to make real financial decisions — job offers, rent vs. buy, retirement — and the tools either cost too much or went stale the week after I made them. So I built something that stays current, because your life does."

**Not like this:**
> "Lever is an AI-powered, MCP-native financial planning platform designed to democratize access to institutional-grade retirement modeling tools."

## What to Emphasize in Community Posts (r/financialindependence, HN, etc.)

- Solo builder. Side project. Real product built for a real problem he had.
- Engineer background — the simulator is deterministic, the math is real, not vibes.
- Not trying to sell financial advice. Sandbox. You own your plan.
- The conversation IS the plan. No forms. No onboarding wizard. Just talk.
- Built on Claude because the AI is genuinely useful, not because "AI" is trendy.

## What NOT to Emphasize

- Corporate language. No "democratize," "leverage," "ecosystem."
- Funding status (side project — lean into it, it's credible).
